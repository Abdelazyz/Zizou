About

I am a PhD candidate at GIPSA-Lab (Grenoble INP – UGA) working in collaboration with ENGIE Green through a CIFRE industrial thesis. My research focuses on developing deep learning methods for acoustic signal processing, with a particular emphasis on wind turbine noise estimation and monitoring.

My work involves designing recurrent neural network architectures to automatically estimate noise levels from acoustic measurements, and exploring the contribution of environmental data (wind speed, direction, temperature) to improve spectrogram reconstruction and noise prediction accuracy.

I am passionate about bridging the gap between signal processing theory and real-world industrial applications in renewable energy and environmental acoustics.
