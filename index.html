About

I am a PhD candidate at GIPSA-Lab (Grenoble INP – UGA) working with ENGIE Green through a CIFRE 'industrial' thesis. My research focuses on developing deep learning models for acoustic signal, with a particular emphasis on wind turbine noise level estimation.

My work focuses on simulating wind turbine acoustic noise under realistic and varying conditions, and on developing deep neural network architectures for the automatic estimation of noise levels from acoustic data.

I am passionate about data science and deep neural networks, with a strong interest in understanding the computations underlying these so-called “black-box” models. I also have a keen interest in teaching mathematics and computer science.
